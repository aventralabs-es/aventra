Classical Technical Analysis

Candlestick Basics

Candlesticks compress price movement into open, high, low, and close. They help traders observe pressure, rejection, continuation, and uncertainty.

Example: Candle pressure and rejection Candles show how price moved inside one period. Wicks, bodies, and closes matter most around important zones.

The long upper wick near resistance shows rejection. That does not automatically mean a reversal, but it tells the trader that the reaction around the zone deserves attention.

What It Is

Each candle shows the range and closing behavior for one period. A large body often suggests directional pressure, while long wicks can show rejection or volatility around a price area.

How Traders Use It

Traders read candles around key zones, trendlines, support, resistance, and structure levels. A candle pattern is more meaningful when it appears at a relevant location rather than in the middle of noise.

Common Mistakes

A common mistake is memorizing candle names without context. A hammer, engulfing candle, or doji has limited value unless the trader understands where it forms and what market condition surrounds it.

Aventra Context

Candlestick reading supports liquidity sweeps, breakout retests, FVG reactions, and volume pressure analysis. It is a small part of a broader decision framework.
